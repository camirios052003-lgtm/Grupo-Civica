# Common Car Problems — Diagnostic Reference

## 🔧 Engine Issues

### Engine Won't Start
| Symptom | Likely Cause | Fix | Cost (USD) | Cost (COP) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Clicking sound, no start | Dead battery | Jump start or replace battery | $100–$250 | $415,000–$1,037,500 |
| Cranks but won't start | Faulty fuel pump or starter | Replace fuel pump / starter | $300–$800 | $1,245,000–$3,320,000 |
| No sound at all | Dead battery or starter motor | Check connections, replace if needed | $150–$500 | $622,500–$2,075,000 |

### Engine Overheating
| Symptom | Likely Cause | Fix | Cost (USD) | Cost (COP) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Temp gauge in red | Low coolant / leaking radiator | Refill coolant or replace radiator | $150–$900 | $622,500–$3,735,000 |
| Steam from hood | Blown head gasket | Head gasket replacement | $1,000–$2,500 | $4,150,000–$10,375,000 |
| Sweet smell from engine | Coolant leak | Locate and seal the leak | $100–$500 | $415,000–$2,075,000 |

### Engine Noises
| Symptom | Likely Cause | Fix | Cost (USD) | Cost (COP) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Knocking sound | Low oil / worn bearings | Oil change or engine rebuild | $50–$3,000+ | $207,500–$12,450,000+ |
| Squealing from belts | Worn serpentine belt | Replace belt | $100–$300 | $415,000–$1,245,000 |
| Ticking sound | Low oil or valve adjustment | Oil top-off or valve adjustment | $50–$400 | $207,500–$1,660,000 |

## 🛞 Brake Problems

| Symptom | Likely Cause | Fix | Cost (USD) | Cost (COP) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Squeaking when braking | Worn brake pads | Replace brake pads | $150–$400 | $622,500–$1,660,000 |
| Grinding sound | Pads completely worn → metal-on-metal | Replace pads + rotors | $300–$800 | $1,245,000–$3,320,000 |
| Car pulls to one side | Uneven brake wear or stuck caliper | Brake inspection + caliper service | $150–$500 | $622,500–$2,075,000 |
| Spongy brake pedal | Air in brake lines or low fluid | Brake fluid flush | $80–$150 | $332,000–$622,500 |
| ABS light on | Faulty ABS sensor | Sensor replacement | $200–$400 | $830,000–$1,660,000 |

**⚠️ CRITICAL**: Brake issues should ALWAYS be treated as HIGH priority.
